Rotating machinery fault diagnosis faces three fundamental challenges under real-world industrial conditions: the time-frequency heterogeneity of vibration signals across varying operating conditions, scarcity of labeled fault samples, and heavy background noise that masks incipient fault signatures. We propose TAFN (Time-Frequency Aware Fusion Network), a dual-stream architecture that jointly models temporal dynamics and spectral patterns through physics-guided priors and entropy-regularized meta-learning. TAFN comprises four key components: a 1D temporal encoder with dilated convolutions for raw waveform analysis, a 2D spectral encoder processing Mel-scaled spectrograms with channel attention, a physics-guided fusion module that injects dimensionless statistical metrics (variance, skewness, kurtosis, clearance factor) as physically meaningful inductive biases, and an entropy-regularized prototypical network for robust few-shot adaptation. Comprehensive experiments on a rotating machinery benchmark demonstrate that TAFN achieves 92.1\% accuracy with only 1.5M parameters, outperforming ViT-B/16 (88.4\%, 86.6M) while using 57$\times$ fewer parameters. Under extreme noise conditions (-8dB SNR), TAFN maintains 61.8\% accuracy -- a 9.7\% absolute improvement over ViT and 16.6\% over CNN baselines. In few-shot scenarios with only 5 samples per class, TAFN reaches 80.5\% accuracy, significantly surpassing MAML (68.5\%) and Prototypical Networks (71.3\%). These results demonstrate that integrating physical priors with deep meta-learning is a promising direction for reliable industrial fault diagnosis under real-world constraints.
